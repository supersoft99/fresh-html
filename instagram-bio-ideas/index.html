100+ Instagram Bio Ideas That You Should Copy

By Jessica MartinezDecember 14, 2021December 17th, 2021No Comments
Read Time: 14 minutes

First impressions matter. A lot. And, as you can imagine, your Instagram bio is all about making a first impression. Today, we are going to look at what makes a bio stand out and how it can directly impact your business (and revenue). Beyond that, we will also look at a ton of bios and ideas that you can copy for your Instagram page. And, finally, we will also explore some cool bio hacks and tricks that you can use to stand apart from the crowd. So, let’s get started!

What is an Instagram Bio?

Your Instagram bio is the small section under your username that you can use to give a quick intro about yourself or your business. Your Instagram bio can include words, hashtags, emojis, website links, and more. Let’s have a look at an example:

In the above example, we have a lot going on despite how simple it may look. The first line ‘Escaped the 9-5’ generates curiosity in the eyes of the viewer. What does he mean, he escaped 9 to 5? The next line ‘Travel & Filmkaing’ makes it immediately clear what the page is all about – ‘Travel’ and ‘Filmmaking’. Next, you have contact info with the ‘let’s chat’ section. And finally, you have a CTA – a call to action. If you also notice, there are multiple emojis within the bio – which makes it lively and colorful instead of plain old text.

The Recipe for the Perfect Instagram Bio

1. Add a Short Description About Yourself

The best Instagram bios are ones that make it instantly clear about what it is you do and who you are. Whether you are an individual, creator, public figure, or business, aim to have a clear and concise description of what you do. It could be your job title, it could be the business you own or the problem you solve as a business, or things you love. Aim for clarity over cleverness. Remember, bios are something that people ‘scan’. They give this section a quick glance. This is why it’s best to keep it super short. Emojis are a great idea as they spice up the space.

Here is what we love about the above example – it’s clear. Within a second, we understand that this business is providing a service for planning Instagram feeds. Now, let’s take a look at another example that we are not too happy with.

At first glance, we are clever but not clear. ‘We provide essential tools to help small businesses get off the ground and grow’. Ummm … what? Yes, most people know what Buffer is all about, for someone who doesn’t, that the first line makes no sense at all.

To sum it up – focus on clarity over cleverness. You’d rather have people immediately get you than not. Here is another example –

‘Actor/Rapper/Singer/Producer’ – well, one thing is for sure, within the first few seconds of landing on this profile, we know exactly what it’s about.

2. List Your Interests and Hobbies

The next step is to give the audience a deeper insight into you. If you’re an individual, this would be where you mention what your interests and hobbies are. You want to connect with your audience. People relate with other people like them. So – whatever it is that interests you, chances are, it interests other people too.

We all belong to a community in some way or another based on our interests. And, you want to connect to your audience based on that interest. Whatever it is you’re into – that’s what you can put in here. It may be that you’re into fitness, or you love taking road trips, or maybe you love reading books.

Here is what we love about this bio. Within the first line of ‘California girl solo traveling the world’ not only we are clear about what the page is about – ‘travel’. We are also getting a deeper insight into the page. It’s a page about a girl from California who travels solo around the world. And this is something the audience can connect with, primarily, female audiences.

The best Instagram bio in our opinion is one that not only gives a clear description of what the page is about but, it also connects with the audience at a different level.

3. Share Your Contact Info

Whether you are a content creator, an artist, an actor, or anything in between – you should always outline a clear path for your audience to connect with you. This takes out the guesswork from your audience. Whether you want people to contact you via DM or you have a specific email for business inquiries – make it clear to the audience. Don’t allow them to get confused as to how they should contact you.

Circling to a previous example, below you can see a clear mention of ‘Let’s chat: business@lostleblanc.com’. This takes out the guesswork. If I am someone who wants to get in touch with this content creator, I know exactly where I should be contacting him.

In our research, we have found that individuals prefer to have a direct email in their bio, whereas, businesses tend to have a CTA to their site which has a ‘contact us’ page. So – if you’re a business, we would definitely suggest focusing more on the CTA rather than having an email in the bio.

4. Have a Call To Action (CTA)

The final part of your bio is where you get to place a link. In most cases, this would be a link to your personal website where the audience can learn everything about you/your service. However, that is not always the case. If you are an artist, you may link to your Spotify. If you are an actor, you may link to your new movie. And, finally, if you are a business, then you would link to your business website.

Here is the fun part about the link in bio – you can utilize the last line of your bio to direct the users towards your CTA link. Now, a CTA (call to action) is usually a text along with a link. Throw in an arrow for direction, and you have a great CTA. Check out the CTA of our site.

 

There are a couple of things going on here. First, we have a text that clearly mentions to users that they can check for eligibility for our service for ‘free’. Next, we have a right-pointing arrow that indicates that you’ll be taken to the next step, should you click on this button. And finally, below the button, there is a tiny text that mentions that we do not require credit card information for you to be able to get an eligibility check for our services.

Following back to our previous examples –

Here is a clear CTA. You have the last line of the bio that has an emoji pointing downward, taking the gaze of the viewer to the link. You have a text that gives insight into the ‘value’ that the link may bring to the audience – ‘marketing tips, downloadables, and more’. Most importantly, notice how they don’t focus on selling their service in their CTA. Rather, they are focusing on giving the viewer value. I mean, if you’re on this page, then you’d definitely enjoy some marketing tips, and perhaps a free e-book on Instagram growth. People are more likely to click on this CTA vs. if they mentioned, ‘check out our app’.

And here we have something similar going on. The first thing that pops up is ‘free’. So, to the user, clicking on this doesn’t mean that they would be taken to a ‘shop’ page. This completely disarms the viewer and allows them to give this link a try. And who knows, maybe they like the site so much, that they turn into a paying customer and a raving fan. But, that comes later. The first step is to get the viewer to click the link. ‘Free Creator Training’ with an arrow to guide the viewer as to where to click, is a perfect example.

Here is what not to do –

Any guesses about what is the problem here? Well – the bio has 5 clickable links. Four of those are ‘@’ tags. And one is a website that directly leads to the ‘pricing’ page. To a viewer, this can become super confusing real quick. Okay, so you’re a fitness coach page that I just landed on, you have tagged four pages in your bio and you have a link URL about pricing, what do I do? Should I click on those four tags one by one and take the time to understand why you have put those four tags? Or, should I directly jump into what your pricing is?

Remember – don’t clutter your bio with multiple CTAs. And, invite people to try you out for ‘free’ or give them ‘resources/guides’ that they can use for free. You have to first build a relation and let the audience know you before you get to the pricing and sale.

5. Format Your Bio

Nothing is more off-putting than having to read a bio that is cluttered with text, random characters, and has no consistency. You know what we are talking about. If we could sum this up in two points, it would be this. First – make sure to use line breaks. It makes it so much easier on the eyes to read a bio that has clear line breaks and lot of white space. And two – use emojis to make your bio lively, concise, and bulleted.
